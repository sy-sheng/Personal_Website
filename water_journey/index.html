Water Journey

- Urban design of coastal community

  • [DATE]   02/2020 - 05/2020
  • [SITE]   Ang Sila, Thailand
  • [AREA]   14.51ha
  • [ADVISOR]   Miho Mazereeuw, MIT Architecture
  • [Personal Work]   Urban design, landscape design, water experience design
  • [Team Work]   Collaborative master plan

  • Water problems including drought, flood, water quality, erosion and other water problems are currently serious in Thailand. With large water area and waterfront area, Thai people attach great importance to water. The importance came from the significance of agricultural production to Thailand as well as local traditional culture.

  • As a coastal village, the site is working closely related to fish farming. The project is trying to propose the possibility of combining the water problem solving with water experience. Try to solve the water problems in a landscape way and give local a better public experience at the same time.
  • Water collecting has always been a problem that Thais try to solve. They continue to propose and imrpove better methods of water collection. The primitive Thai people use the slope of the roof to collect more rainwater for everyday life. With the change of housing form, people adape the method to morden housing and add water storage and purification module after the collecting. Currently, new technology and circulation systems are applied. The water cllected in can be reused for the house or exchange with government water sypply system.
  • Different plantings are also providing different landscape scene, creating different environment and atmosphere for different activities. This can provide people with different tour experiences.

  • The different scenarios in the drawing show the tour sequence with the local planting species suggested.
  • The pocket parks between buildings are using small seperated araas to purify water and create more activity spaces for locals at the same time. The spaces are seperated in different courtyards. Without using them, they are just being left and difficult to organize. In most of the situations, they are just the empty spaces left between buildings. With those pocket parks, there are venues for people gathering and organizing activities.

  • The water purification in this step is more hidden. Peole do not have much close contact with the water but can enjoy the wetland view in the courtyard with the mini-wetland created with the collected water.

  • Building an eco-system to habitating the cleaned water can relive the water and let the water breed the plants. The plants can build up the eco-system and give local wield animals new habitats. In this way, at the same time with habitating, the wetland park can creat different water interactive experiece for the visitors.
  • The coastal water experience is a combination of coastal experience and brakish water. While it is a coastal area that can experience the change brought by the sea, it is also close to the brakish wetland. It is a great chance to expand local activities and create a space for gathering activities. Thus, the coastal experience is organized with a building of multi-functions and multi-experience.

  • The main tour of the building is highlighed. The tour shows the different views including brakish wetland and the seaside, underground and in the trees which can offer visitors a rich tour experience. At the same time, the building itself is a circulation system, the water is collected, purified and reused inside the building which is a great chance to create an educational base for water sustainability education. The interaction with the surroudnings also eliminates the sense of boundary between the building and the surrounding envrionment; reconnecting the indoor spaces with the nature can remind people their connection with the nature.
